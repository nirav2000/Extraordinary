# Young High Flyers - Content Pack

## 1) Positioning

**Working title:** Young High Flyers  
**One-line promise:** A curated collection of young people who achieved something extraordinary early - and why their stories matter.  
**Editorial angle:** Not just prodigy worship. The project focuses on pattern recognition: curiosity, access, leverage, discipline, moral courage, media fluency, and the role of luck, mentorship and institutions.

## 2) Homepage copy

### Hero headline
Young High Flyers

### Hero subhead
A cross-category collection of extraordinary young people in science, sport, entrepreneurship, activism and culture - with short notes on what each case teaches us.

### Intro paragraph
Most lists of prodigies are shallow. This project tries to do something more useful: build a resource you can browse, search and write from. Each profile is paired with a compact "why it matters" insight, so the collection works as a database for essays, newsletters, interviews, scouting and product ideas.

## 3) Category blurbs

### Science & Engineering
Young people whose technical curiosity turned into experiments, prototypes, discoveries or systems-thinking. This category includes both constructive role models and one deliberate cautionary case.

### Entrepreneurship & Tech
Teenagers and young founders who shipped products, built audiences, launched companies or defined new technical architectures.

### Activism & Impact
Young people whose influence came through courage, organization, testimony or movement-building rather than inventions or money.

### Arts, Culture & Writing
Creators whose work mattered early - whether through composition, literature, performance, painting or digital publishing.

### Sports
Athletes who broke through against elite competition early enough to permanently change what seemed normal for their age.

## 4) Three featured editorial lenses

### Lens A - Curiosity with and without guardrails
Use David Hahn and Taylor Wilson side by side. Both were fascinated by nuclear science. One story shows reckless concentration of dangerous materials without proper oversight. The other shows what happens when advanced interest meets labs, mentors and safety culture.

### Lens B - Small acts that scale
Mari Copeny, Greta Thunberg, Ryan Hreljac and Marley Dias all started with actions that were legible and repeatable: a letter, a strike, a fundraising goal, a book drive.

### Lens C - Youth authority in the internet era
Tavi Gevinson, Billie Eilish, Coco Gauff, Michael Sayman and Ben Pasternak each illustrate a different form of early internet-native visibility.

## 7) Suggested "About" page copy

Young High Flyers is a research-and-curation project focused on early achievement. The aim is not to romanticize pressure or reduce people to their youngest accomplishment. Instead, the project studies patterns: how unusual talent emerges, how opportunity compounds, how institutions help or fail, and what today's young people can teach us about the future.
